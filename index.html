Online Safety Education

Understanding how online predators operate is an important step in protecting yourself and others.

How grooming begins

Predators often start by building trust offering compliments gifts or emotional support to create a false sense of safety.

Warning signs to watch for

Sudden secrecy requests to move conversations to private platforms or attempts to isolate a minor from friends and family are common red flags.

Protecting your accounts

Use strong passwords enable two factor authentication and avoid sharing personal information with strangers online.

What to do if you suspect grooming

Document the behavior avoid confrontation and report the individual to a trusted adult platform moderators or law enforcement.

If you're struggling

If you or someone you know is feeling overwhelmed unsafe or emotionally distressed you do not have to face it alone. Reaching out to a trained professional or someone you trust can make a real difference.

• Talk to a trusted friend family member teacher or mentor.
• Reach out to a licensed mental health professional.
• Contact a local crisis hotline or emergency service in your area.
• If you ever feel in immediate danger seek emergency help right away.
